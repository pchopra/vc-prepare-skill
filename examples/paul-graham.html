How They Think

Paul Graham evaluates in the first 60 seconds. He fires rapid questions to understand what you actually do, not what you think you do. He values conciseness over polish: if you can't explain VC Whisper in one sentence, he'll assume you don't understand your own product. He gets excited about tools that remove friction from processes everyone accepts as painful, and he probes hard on 'why now' and 'why you.' He famously hates slides. Just have a conversation.

Expand beyond prep

AI-native, not AI-wrapped
VC Whisper isn't a ChatGPT wrapper. It orchestrates multiple AI services (Exa for search, xAI for tweets, Browserbase for scraping) with domain-specific prompt engineering. PG distinguishes between 'uses AI' and 'is AI.'

Predicted Questions

“What do you actually do?”
One sentence: 'Founders enter an investor's name, and we generate a complete briefing: their career, portfolio, relevant tweets, and a game plan for the meeting.' Then shut up and let him ask follow-ups.
“Why would someone pay for this when they can just Google the investor?”
Because Googling takes 2 hours and misses the connections. VC Whisper cross-references their portfolio, recent tweets, and your specific company in 60 seconds. Show the Paul Graham example output. The depth speaks for itself.
“What's the bigger play here?”
Fundraising is broken. Founders waste months meeting wrong-fit investors. We started with meeting prep because it's the sharpest pain point. Next: investor discovery (who should you even be talking to), then CRM, then the full fundraising stack. Every founder raises money; the TAM is every startup.
“How do you make money?”
Freemium. Free tier with basic briefings. Pro tier ($49/mo) with API-enriched data, PDF exports, unlimited briefings. Enterprise tier for accelerators and law firms who do this for their portfolio companies.

Do Not Mention

Don't say 'AI-powered' without substance
PG has been publicly skeptical of startups that lead with 'AI' as a differentiator. Lead with the problem (founders are unprepared for investor meetings) and the result (a complete briefing in 60 seconds). Let him discover the AI is sophisticated, don't pitch it.
Don't compare to LinkedIn Sales Navigator or Crunchbase
PG thinks in terms of novel categories, not competitive positioning against incumbents. VC Whisper isn't a better database; it's a new workflow. Comparing to existing tools makes it feel incremental.
